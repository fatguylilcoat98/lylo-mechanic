The Four Layers

Four ways you get protected. One place to look.

Most diagnostic apps give you a code and a guess. LYLO runs your scan through four independent layers — each designed to catch a different way people get taken advantage of at a repair shop.

T
LAYER 01
Truth Detector
Catches HIDDEN problems

Someone cleared the codes before you drove it off the lot. Readiness monitors haven't completed. Freeze-frame data exists but no active codes. The car is trying to tell you something was erased.

Example: Seller says "no issues, clean scan." LYLO detects codes were cleared 3 days ago and 4 readiness monitors are incomplete. Warns you to walk away.
H
LAYER 02
Failure Predictor
Catches COMING problems

Weak battery voltage. Coolant creeping up. Fuel trims drifting. The car is compensating for a problem that hasn't triggered a warning light yet. LYLO sees it days or weeks before it becomes a breakdown.

Example: Battery reads 11.9V. No check-engine light yet. LYLO warns: "This battery may fail within 2-4 weeks, especially in cold weather. Schedule a test."
B
LAYER 03
Event Black Box
Catches PAST problems

Hard brakes. Rapid acceleration. Overspeed. Possible impacts. LYLO keeps a 30-second rolling buffer so if something happens, you have proof of what the car was doing before, during, and after.

Example: Teen driver hit something. Vehicle went from 45 mph to near-zero in one second. LYLO logs the event with peak values and surrounding snapshots.
Q
LAYER 04
Quote Auditor
Catches INVENTED problems

A mechanic says "you need a new catalytic converter, $1,800." You type that into LYLO. LYLO cross-references the quote against your actual OBD data and tells you plainly: supported or not supported. You get a script to read at the counter.

Example: Shop quotes $1,800 for a new cat. Your scan shows no P0420, no O2 anomaly, fuel trims normal. LYLO says: "The data does NOT support this. Ask the mechanic to show you the fault code that led to it."

If something is hiding the truth, we show it.
If something is starting to fail, we warn you.
If something happened, we prove it.
If something invented a problem, we expose it.
